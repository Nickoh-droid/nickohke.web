My First Day in College — The Wrong Class

Published on March 5, 2025

It was my first day of class at Mukiria Technical Training Institute, and everything felt new and exciting. The timetable showed “Lab 1,” but I had no idea where that was. I decided to ask a friendly-looking comrade for directions, and he confidently pointed me toward a computer lab.

Once I got there, I saw computers all around and thought, “This must be it.” Without a second thought, I entered, took out my pen and notebook, and began to write what was on the board — “Web Development.” Everyone was quiet, listening attentively to the teacher. It felt just right.

But then the teacher stopped and looked directly at me. “Hey, young man, where have you come from?” he asked. I froze. I didn’t know what to say. “Do you belong here?” he continued. I said yes, though I wasn’t sure. He asked if I knew anyone in the room — and of course, I didn’t. The whole class went silent.

The teacher smiled slightly and said, “You might be in the wrong class.” My heart dropped. I looked around; everyone was staring at me. He told me to stand, pack my things, and find my correct class. The worst part? He didn’t even tell me where it was. I walked out, embarrassed, not knowing where to go next.

Still, that experience taught me something real about college life — that it’s not like high school. You’re expected to find your way, to ask questions, and to take responsibility for your path. It was an awkward start, but it became a story I’ll never forget.
